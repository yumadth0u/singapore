☀ Weather — July / August

Hot and humid, 30–34°C with afternoon rain showers (usually 30–60 min, then bright again). Plan outdoor activities in the mornings and indoor / air-conditioned from 1–4pm. Always carry a compact umbrella. Bring a pop-up UV beach tent for the baby.

🦟 Mosquitoes — Yes, Present

Singapore has Aedes mosquitoes (dengue risk). Most prevalent in parks, gardens and Mandai wildlife reserves — especially at dawn and dusk.

Baby (4 months): Use a pram mosquito net. Apply picaridin/icaridin repellent (Autan Family or Mosi-guard Natural) — safer than DEET for infants. Light long sleeves in parks.

Everyone else: DEET (25–30%) or picaridin, reapplied every few hours. Guardian and Watsons pharmacies stock repellents everywhere.

Most vigilant at: Gardens by the Bay, Mandai, Botanic Gardens, Sentosa beach vegetation at dusk.

Best Hawker Centres

◆ What is a hawker centre?

Open-air food halls where independent stalls serve freshly cooked Singapore street food for SGD 3–8 a dish. Many stalls have been cooking the same recipe for 30–40 years. This is how most Singaporeans eat — and it's some of the best food in the world.

🏆 Lau Pa Sat

Raffles Quay · 5-min Grab · Open 24 hrs

A stunning Victorian cast-iron building (1894) in the CBD. At night, the street outside becomes Satay Street — rows of smoking charcoal satay skewers.

  • Satay (SGD 0.80/skewer) — must-order
  • Oyster omelette · Roti prata · Teh tarik
Best for: Evening after Marina Bay walk

🐔 Maxwell Food Centre

Chinatown · 10-min Grab · 8am–10pm

Singapore's most famous hawker. Home to Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice (stall 10) — the dish Gordon Ramsay queued for.

  • Tian Tian Chicken Rice — SGD 4–6
  • Zhen Zhen Porridge · China Street Fritters
Best for: Lunch on Day 5 (Chinatown)

🌿 Satay by the Bay

Gardens by the Bay · No extra travel · 5pm–midnight

Outdoor hawker right inside Gardens by the Bay with Supertree views. Very family-friendly — pram access, open space for kids to run.

  • Satay · BBQ seafood · Nasi lemak
  • Ice kacang (shaved ice) — kids love it
Best for: Dinner on Day 2 — zero extra travel

🌊 Makansutra Gluttons Bay

Esplanade Waterfront · 10-min walk · 6pm–late

Curated outdoor hawker with stunning Marina Bay views. One of the most scenic places to eat street food anywhere in Asia.

  • Oyster omelette · BBQ sambal stingray
  • Char kway teow · Chilli crab bun
Best for: Any evening — walking distance from Conrad

🏡 Tiong Bahru Market

Tiong Bahru · 15-min Grab · 6am–8pm

Local neighbourhood institution — less touristy than Maxwell, more authentic. Great for a lazy morning brunch.

  • Tiong Bahru Char Kway Teow — legendary
  • Chwee Kueh · Bao Today buns · Popiah
Best for: Free-morning brunch

✈ Old Airport Road Food Centre

Geylang · 20-min Grab · 6am–midnight

Regarded by many locals as the best hawker centre in Singapore. Enormous choice, long-established stalls, incredible quality.

  • Toa Payoh Roast Duck · Hup Kee Oyster Omelette
  • Wei Yi Laksa · Ice Ball desserts
Best for: Dedicated hawker evening

👶 Hawker Tips with Kids & Pram

  • Pram access: All the above are step-free. Lau Pa Sat, Satay by the Bay and Makansutra are the most spacious.
  • Tissue packet = table is taken — one adult grabs a table, others queue at stalls. Local custom.
  • Drinks: Barley water (cold, mild, kids love it) or fresh coconut.
  • Timing: 11:30am or 5:30pm to beat the rush.
  • Budget: Full meal for 4 adults + 3 children: SGD 60–90 total (approx. £35–53).

Your group spans meat, pescatarian and vegetarian. The restaurants below either cater to all three simultaneously, or are so exceptional for one dietary type they merit a dedicated visit. The Wagyu picks below all have seafood and vegetarian alternatives on the same menu.

The Cavern Restaurant — Singapore's Only Cave Dining

🪨 Cavern Restaurant — Rainforest Wild ASIA, Mandai

Mandai Wildlife Reserve · Lunch 11am–3pm · Dinner 5pm–9pm · No park admission required

Singapore's first and only restaurant built inside a genuine cave setting, inspired by the Mulu Caves of Sarawak. Towering rock formations, crystal lighting mimicking cave droplets, and a seating capacity of 158. Managed by TungLok Group with Head Chef Sheng Lim.

The menu is international Western — not a hawker, not a food court. Think well-executed mains, pastas and desserts in a genuinely extraordinary setting.

  • Weekday lunch sets from SGD 28++ (2-course) / SGD 35++ (3-course)
  • Best combined with Day 4 (Zoo/River Wonders) or Day 7 (Bird Paradise) — already at Mandai
  • Kids go absolutely wild for the cave environment
  • Vegetarian mains available on the menu

Evening Shows

💧 Spectra — Marina Bay Sands Promenade

MBS Event Plaza · FREE · Nightly ~8pm & 9pm · 15 min

A free outdoor light, water and laser show along the Marina Bay Sands waterfront. Dazzling water jets synchronized with music and coloured lasers. 10-min walk from the Conrad — the most accessible show of the trip.

  • No tickets, no booking — just show up
  • Best viewing: MBS Promenade (free side) or Helix Bridge
  • Stroller-friendly flat waterfront path
Best for: Any evening · Post-dinner stroll · Free

🌳 Garden Rhapsody — Gardens by the Bay

Supertree Grove · FREE · 7:45pm & 8:45pm nightly · 10 min

The Supertrees come alive with a synchronised light and music show. Standing at the base of the 50m trees looking up is genuinely one of Singapore's most magical experiences. All four children will remember this.

  • Free — no booking needed
  • Pay to walk the OCBC Skyway suspended bridge between Supertrees (~SGD 14 adult, SGD 10 child)
  • Combine with Day 2 visit or any evening trip back to the gardens
Best for: Day 2 evening · Free · Kids love it

🎆 Wings of Time — Sentosa

Siloso Beach · SGD 22–28pp · 7:40pm & 8:40pm nightly · 20 min

Singapore's only nightly outdoor fireworks show over the sea — now with 4× longer fireworks since Feb 2025. Water screen projections, dancing fountains, laser magic and fire bursts. A proper spectacle.

  • Book online — discounts available vs. gate price
  • Combine with Day 3 (Sentosa beach day) — stay for the evening show
  • Infant in pram: fine, open-air amphitheatre seating
Best for: Day 3 evening · Kids' favourite show of the trip

🦉 Night Safari — Mandai

Mandai · SGD 55 adult / SGD 38 child · Opens 6:15pm

The world's first nocturnal zoo — explore 8 km of trails in near-darkness as animals are most active. The tram circuit rides past free-roaming animals inches away. Genuinely unlike anything else on the trip.

  • Creatures of the Night show at the amphitheatre (owls, binturong, civets)
  • Best combined with Day 4 (already at Mandai) — stay for Night Safari after dinner
  • Bring mosquito repellent; pram can navigate the main tram route
Best for: Day 4 extension · Spectacular for kids 5+

Instagram & TikTok Spots

◆ Most-photographed spots in Singapore right now

Many of these are already in the itinerary — here's where to make time for photos specifically.

📸 Haji Lane

Arab Street · 10-min Grab · Any time, best 9–11am

Singapore's most-photographed street. Paint-splashed shophouses, bold murals, neon signs and an intensely colourful, narrow alley. Viral on TikTok constantly. Boutique cafés and shops line every wall.

Add: 1hr detour on Day 5 (Cultural day) — 10 min from Little India

📸 Joo Chiat / Katong Shophouses

East Singapore · 20-min Grab

Rows of pastel-painted Peranakan heritage shophouses — the most beautiful streetscape in Singapore. Endless content. Joo Chiat Road and Koon Seng Road are the best-known rows.

Add: Morning detour on any free morning

📸 Old Hill Street Police Station

Clarke Quay · 10-min walk from Conrad

A colonial-era building covered in 927 rainbow-hued windows — one of the most iconic and viral photo spots in all of Singapore. Free, always accessible, 2 minutes from Clarke Quay.

Add: Pass on the way to/from Clarke Quay dinner (Day 5)

📸 Jewel Changi Rain Vortex

Changi Airport Terminal 1 · Departure day

The world's tallest indoor waterfall (40m) cascading through a glass dome surrounded by a forest. Absolutely stunning and constantly viral. Already in your itinerary — departure day at the airport.

Already planned: Departure morning

📸 OCBC Skyway — Supertrees

Gardens by the Bay · SGD 14 adult / SGD 10 child

The suspended walkway between two 50m Supertrees, 22m above the ground. Looking down at the Gardens and back at the Marina Bay skyline. Phenomenal photo opportunity, especially at golden hour.

Add to Day 2 (Gardens visit) — go just before Garden Rhapsody

📸 Henderson Waves Bridge

Southern Ridges · 15-min Grab · Sunset / early evening

Singapore's highest pedestrian bridge (36m) — an undulating wave-form structure that looks extraordinary. Connects to the Canopy Walk through the forest. Best at dusk or just after sunset for the lights.

Add: Optional late-afternoon detour any day

📸 Marina Bay Waterfront at Night

10-min walk from Conrad · Free · Best after 7pm

The full Marina Bay skyline reflected in the water — MBS, Esplanade, Singapore Flyer, Supertrees all in one frame. The classic Singapore shot. Walk from the Conrad along the promenade.

Already in itinerary — do it every evening you're near

📸 Tiong Bahru Murals

Tiong Bahru · 15-min Grab · Best 8–10am

A 1930s housing estate covered in whimsical Ernest Zacharevic murals — cats, children playing, street scenes. Pair with Tiong Bahru Market brunch for a perfect local morning.

Add: Combine with a hawker morning (Tiong Bahru Market nearby)

🪁 Marina Barrage (morning)🌺 Botanic Gardens (afternoon)💰 ~SGD 60 (mostly free)

Morning — Marina Barrage

09:00
Marina Bay Waterfront Bike Ride Optional
Rent bikes from CityScoot at the Esplanade or grab-and-go via the Anywheel / SG Bike dock-less apps. The flat waterfront circuit runs from Esplanade past Merlion, Marina Bay Sands, Helix Bridge, Gardens by the Bay and back — about 5–8 km, 45–60 min. Child seats available for younger children; baby can ride in a carrier or pram and be left at Esplanade while adults and older kids ride. A guided night cycling tour (Viator/Klook, ~SGD 40pp, departs 7pm) is also brilliant if you prefer to ride after the heat drops.
10:30
Marina Barrage — Rooftop Garden & Kite Flying Free Pram ✓
One of Singapore's most underrated family spots. A 10-min Grab from the Conrad (~SGD 12). The green rooftop garden has panoramic 360° views of the entire Marina Bay skyline. Pick up an inexpensive kite from Daiso (SGD 2–5) and the older children will be thrilled. Ground-level Visitor Centre open 9am–5pm.

Afternoon — Singapore Botanic Gardens

14:00
Jacob Ballas Children's Garden Free Pram ✓
Dedicated section of the UNESCO World Heritage Botanic Gardens — entirely free, shaded, designed for children up to 12. Water play, treehouse, suspension bridge. Apply mosquito repellent before entering.
16:00 optional
National Orchid Garden
Over 1,000 orchid species including Singapore's national flower. Adult ~SGD 15, children free.
