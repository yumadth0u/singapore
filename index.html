☀️ Weather — July / August

Hot and humid, 30–34°C with afternoon rain showers (usually 30–60 min, then bright again). Plan outdoor activities in the mornings and indoor/air-conditioned activities from 1–4pm. Always carry a compact umbrella. Bring a pop-up UV beach tent for the baby for any outdoor time.

🦟 Mosquitoes — Yes, They Are Present

Singapore has Aedes mosquitoes, which can carry dengue. The government actively controls them, but they are a genuine presence in parks, gardens and the Mandai wildlife reserves — especially at dawn and dusk. City centre, hotel areas and malls have far fewer.

For the baby (4 months): Use a pram mosquito net — essential for all park visits. Apply a picaridin/icaridin-based repellent (Autan Family or Mosi-guard Natural) to exposed skin — safer than DEET for infants. Never apply to hands or near eyes. Dress baby in light long sleeves and trousers in parks.

For everyone else: Standard DEET (25–30%) or picaridin repellent, reapplied every few hours. Light-coloured clothing helps. Guardian and Watsons pharmacies across Singapore stock repellents if you forget to pack them.

Most vigilant at: Gardens by the Bay, Mandai Zoo & Bird Paradise, Botanic Gardens, Sentosa beach vegetation at dusk.

🍜 Singapore's Best Hawker Centres

💡 What is a hawker centre?

Open-air or covered food halls where dozens of independent stalls sell freshly cooked Singapore street food for as little as SGD 3–8 a dish. Many stalls have been cooking the same recipe for 30–40 years. This is how most Singaporeans eat — and it's some of the best food in the world.

🏆 Lau Pa Sat

Raffles Quay · 5-min Grab from Conrad · Open 24 hrs

A stunning Victorian cast-iron building (1894) in the CBD. One of Singapore's most iconic hawker venues. At night, the street outside becomes Satay Street — row upon row of smoking charcoal satay skewers.

  • Satay (SGD 0.80/skewer) — must-order
  • Oyster omelette (char kway) — stall 28
  • Roti prata with curry
  • Teh tarik (pulled milk tea)

Best for: Evening after Marina Bay walk — combine with the light show

🐔 Maxwell Food Centre

Chinatown · 10-min Grab from Conrad · Daily 8am–10pm

Singapore's most famous hawker centre. Home to Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice (stall 10) — the dish Gordon Ramsay queued for. Expect a 20-min wait but absolutely worth it.

  • Tian Tian Chicken Rice — SGD 4–6 (stall 10)
  • Zhen Zhen Porridge — perfect for little ones
  • China Street Fritters — tofu, egg fritters
  • Maxwell Fuzhou Oyster Cake

Best for: Lunch on Day 5 (Chinatown day) — it's right there

🌿 Satay by the Bay

Gardens by the Bay · No extra travel needed · 5pm–12am (Fri/Sat later)

Outdoor hawker right inside Gardens by the Bay, with views of the Supertrees. Perfect open-air setting for an evening meal after the light show. Very family-friendly — pram access, open space for kids to run.

  • Satay — the best outdoor satay experience
  • BBQ seafood (stingray, tiger prawns)
  • Nasi lemak, char kway teow
  • Ice kacang (shaved ice dessert) — kids love it

Best for: Dinner on Day 2 (Gardens by the Bay day) — zero extra travel

🌊 Makansutra Gluttons Bay

Esplanade Waterfront · 10-min walk from Conrad · Daily 6pm–late

A curated outdoor hawker at the Esplanade waterfront with stunning Marina Bay views. Only about 12 stalls but each one is an award-winner. One of the most scenic places to eat street food anywhere in Asia.

  • Oyster omelette — the signature dish here
  • BBQ sambal stingray
  • Char kway teow (stir-fried noodles)
  • Chilli crab bun (easier than full crab for little ones)

Best for: Any evening — walking distance from Conrad, stunning sunset views

🏡 Tiong Bahru Market

Tiong Bahru · 15-min Grab from Conrad · Daily 6am–8pm

A local neighbourhood institution in Singapore's oldest housing estate. Two floors — wet market below, hawker upstairs. Less touristy than Maxwell, more authentic. Great for a lazy morning brunch.

  • Tiong Bahru Char Kway Teow — legendary queue, worth it
  • Chwee Kueh (steamed rice cakes with preserved radish)
  • Bao Today — freshly baked bao buns, kids adore them
  • Popiah fresh spring rolls

Best for: Morning brunch on a free morning — pair with a stroll round the Art Deco neighbourhood

✈️ Old Airport Road Food Centre

Geylang area · 20-min Grab from Conrad · Daily 6am–midnight

Built on Singapore's original 1930s airport site, this is regarded by many locals as the best hawker centre in Singapore. Enormous choice, long-established stalls, incredible quality. Worth the slightly longer Grab.

  • Toa Payoh Lor 8 Roast Duck — crispy perfection
  • Hup Kee Fried Oyster Omelette
  • Wei Yi Laksa (stall 01-112) — rich coconut curry noodles
  • Ice Ball desserts — kids will be obsessed

Best for: Dedicated hawker evening — a real local experience away from tourist spots

👶 Hawker Centre Tips with Kids & a Pram

  • Pram access: All the above have step-free access. Lau Pa Sat, Satay by the Bay, and Makansutra are the most spacious.
  • Order at the stall, then sit: One adult secures a table with a tissue packet or bag (this is how it's done), others queue at stalls. Tissue packet = table is taken — it's a local custom!
  • Baby-friendly dishes: Plain congee/porridge (available at most centres) is ideal for a 4-month-old if you've started solids by then. Otherwise, feed baby before you go.
  • Drinks: Always get barley water — cold, mild, kids love it. Or fresh coconut. Avoid tap water; stick to sealed bottles.
  • Timing: Go at 11:30am or 5:30pm to beat the lunch/dinner rush. Midweek is quieter than weekends.
  • Budget: A full meal for your group of 4 adults + 3 children is easily done for SGD 60–90 total (≈ £35–53) — extraordinary value.

Morning — Marina Barrage

09:00

Marina Barrage — Rooftop Garden & Kite Flying Free 🛒

One of Singapore's most underrated family spots, and very few tourists know about it. A 10-min Grab from the Conrad (~SGD 12). The Marina Barrage is a dam that created the Marina Reservoir — but the real draw is the stunning green rooftop garden with panoramic 360° views of the entire Marina Bay skyline (the same skyline you've been looking at from the hotel, but from a completely different angle at water level). Locals bring kites to fly up here — pick up an inexpensive kite from any Daiso or sports shop beforehand (SGD 2–5) and the older children will have a brilliant time. The rooftop is entirely flat, wide and pram-perfect. The Visitor Centre at ground level (open daily, 9am–5pm) explains Singapore's water story through interactive exhibits — the 7 and 9-year-olds find it genuinely interesting. There is also a small café on-site. Apply mosquito repellent before going outdoors.
